  Recommendations for a more professional, symmetrical homepage:

  1. Dedicated "quick action" hero button
  The "How are you feeling today?" check-in button could be larger and more visually distinct — a full-width card with a gradient background
  and a subtitle like "Your last check-in was 2 days ago." This creates a clear primary call-to-action at the top before the grid.

  2. Enforce even card counts in 2-column grids
  Even numbers (2, 4, 6) always produce a clean grid with no orphaned card. Currently the young person dashboard has 6 (perfect). Admin has 4
  after your change (perfect). Trainee has 4 (perfect). You could pair the two "Help and Support" orphan-risk cards with something purposeful.

  3. Group cards into labelled sections
  Instead of one flat grid, divide into two named rows with a small heading:

  ▎ Your tools → Check-In · Review · Patterns · Resources
  ▎ Support → Session Companion · Help

  This adds visual structure and guides the eye down the page naturally.

  4. Add a thin "at a glance" stats strip between the banner and the cards
  A row of 3 small stat chips (e.g., "Check-ins this week: 4", "Streak: 3 days", "Last mood: 7/10") gives the page something dynamic and
  personalised without clutter. It makes the dashboard feel alive rather than a static menu.

  5. Make the welcome banner taller / more spacious
  Increasing its padding slightly and adding a subtle illustration or pattern to the right side (purely decorative SVG waves or dots) would
  make the top section feel much more like a real product landing area.
